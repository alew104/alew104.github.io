The Problem

CarMax is trying to drive new users to use their website to find cars. They have found that their current users have dropped and thus want to redesign their information architecuture and site map to accomodate more users.

What is it?

In this redesign, we propose that CarMax should add more social media integration, as well as offering a new path to desired results. We propose that CarMax design a wizard that will allow users to develop a candidate list of cars. Our persona, a young college student, is able to explore cars and narrow down their list of cars. We also offer social media integration so that they can share their car choices to their friends.

Process

As a team, we first interviewed several college students about the way they shopped for cars. As our interviews showed us, we found that college students typically didn't know that much about cars, wanted something that fit their lifestyle, and wants to be able to research cars online. We also found that they typically kept their parents concerns in mind.

After interviewing, we consolidated our data and designed a user flow and an info flow. We also looked at other competitor websites such as edmunds.com and cars.com. As we deemed that these students were typically tech-savvy, we assumed that they knew of other websites and how to research them, so we wanted to make sure our designs kept other features that other websites may have had in mind.

Using this information, we deemed that the wizard would be the best way to lead inexperienced students into building a candidate list of cars. We designed the social media pages, the wizard process, and the final database schema that would revolve around the CarMax website.
